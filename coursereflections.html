Over the course of the semester, I have gained skills using several web-based technologies including:

·         FotoFlexer / Google Web Album–  an online image editing software, and an online photo storage sotware

·         Wordpress – a blog site

·         GoogleSites – an online resource for building websites from templates

·         SpicyNodes – an online tool for creating interactive outlines for different projects

·         Jing – a screen capture software used for creating short videos and screenshot clips

·         Kompozer – a free website software used to build websites from scratch

Of these tools, Kompozer is by far my favorite and the one I think I excelled in the most. I typically enjoy anything that allows me to use creative design, and this is no exception. I would build a website from scratch using Kompozer ten times before I’d willingly build one in GoogleSites from a template. It is time consuming for me, mainly because I’m a perfectionist and I do all of my own design work on things like backgrounds, banners and buttons, using Photoshop rather than the websites that generate those things for me. I like being able to use those skills to come up with a unique design.

If I had the option to eliminate any project, it would have been FotoFlexer or GoogleSites. I struggled with both of these. FotoFlexer was not difficult to use, but it was frustrating to me because I am so used to Photoshop and its capabilities. I felt like my hands were tied; however, I think it’s a great tool for others who don’t have any experience or access to Photoshop. GoogleSites was challenging for me because I found it difficult to navigate. Just changing the color of text or backgrounds seemed to take a ridiculous amount of clicks and steps, and required navigation to other webpages.

I hope to be able to build on the knowledge I’ve gained from this class, especially in the area of web design. As much as I don’t ever want to look at it again, I know I would benefit from becoming better acquainted with GoogleSites. From what I’ve been told, it’s very likely that I will be required to use this in the classroom because it’s a free for my students to use at home.

The greatest thing about these tools is that they are free. As a Business Technology Education major in Mississippi, there’s no doubt that funding for software will be hard to come by in most areas. Even if the software is available at school, I think it’s great that my students could use each of these resources at home too, as long as they have internet, without having to purchase expensive software.

Technology is not going away and I want my students to leave my classroom as well equipped as humanly possible. It never hurts to be a few steps ahead of the game and with these skills, that’s completely possible.
