GoogleSites

GoogleSites was a new experience for me, and one I hope I never have to endure again. I found it to be extremely annoying and the exact opposite of user friendly. In my opinion there is no good reason for a user to have to go to a completely different page just to change the color of text, headings, backgrounds, etc. Another issue I had was with the templates. At first, it seemed as if the templates were fairly customizable with different color schemes and options, but when you try to change those things, you’re told that it’s not allowed with the free version and that you must upgrade to a premium version. Those two factors combined make GoogleSites repelling, especially knowing that I can use Wix or Weebly to create a nicer looking website with less effort for free. The only thing I like about this software is that it’s readily available in my Google tool center.  I am a huge Google fan and use lots of their web-tools like Drive, Mail, Maps, Calendar, Contacts and YouTube. GoogleSites, sadly, won’t become a frequently used Google too for me unless some big changes are made. It has potential to be a valuable tool, but there are too many other options out there that do the job better for the same price.

Kompozer


Kompozer has been, by far, my favorite tool to work with this semester. I have spent more time looking at its interface than all the others combined, I would bet, but it’s been much more enjoyable. I love being able to use my own mind to create my own designs. I’m not the most creative person in the world, but I do know how to use creative tools which helps a lot. The first website I created, the instructional site on Photoshop, took me two full days to complete. Most of that time was spent perfecting my layout, then creating the videos. I was much more pleased with the outcome of it than the other website tool though. I think Kompozer is fairly easy to use but I have had a couple of issues with text not wanting to be adjusted. There have been several occasions on this site where I’ve tried to change the color, font or other attribute of a line of text but it wouldn’t responds. I finally learned to save and close the document and then reopen it. Usually, when I reopen it everything works fine for a while. It’s not a huge issue, but it can be frustrating and rather time consuming.

I don’t really care to learn about code and such, so this is the type of software I am happy using. I also think it would be fairly easy to teach students to use. Any student who is proficient with Microsoft Word should have very little trouble adapting to this.
